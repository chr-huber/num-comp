{% blocktrans trimmed %} As in Stage 1, you will have 3 minutes’ time in order to correctly solve as many addition exercises as possible. However, your payment in this stage depends on your performance relative to the performance of a group of participants. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Allocation in groups" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Each group consists of 5 participants, all of whom are men. Groups are randomly formed at the beginning of this stage and each participant stays in the same group until the end of Part 1. You will not find out the identity of the other participants in your group during or after the experiment, so that all decisions remain anonymous. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Payment" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} If Stage 2 is the stage selected for payment (in Part 1), then your payment depends on how many additions you have solved correctly in comparison with the other four participants in your group. The two group members who have entered the most correct answers are the two winners of the tournament. The two winners receive €1.50 per correct answer each, while the other three members do not receive any payment. In case of a tie, the ranking among the members with equal performances is determined randomly. From now on, we call this method of payment the Tournament payment. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} You will not be informed about the outcome of the tournament until the end of the experiment. {% endblocktrans %}
